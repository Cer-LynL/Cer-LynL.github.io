Two very common houseplants

Monstera Deliciosa

Basic needs

Watering need Light need Soil type
Allow the soil to dry out between waterings Bright indirect light (east facing window) Loamy, well-draining

Monstera Deliciosa is a tropical plant from the Araceae family. Although it is native to Central and South America, it is typically grown as a houseplant. Monstera is quite easy to take care of. You only need to make sure you follow the few requirements it has. Two most important would be giving it enough light and also proper watering. What to bare in mind is that they tend to grow quite long. When in nature they grow up to 20 meters then indoors is luckly different, generally between 2-3 meters. When taken good care of, Monsteras bring great joy and beauty to your home.

Zanzibar Gem

Basic needs

Watering need Light need Soil type
Allow the soil to become dry at the top to the touch between watering and do not over watert Bright light is good but not essential Well-draining potting mix with sand

Similar to Monstera, the "ZZ plant" also comes from Central and South America. When Monstera needs watering quite often because it tends to dry out fast, then Zanzibar can be unwatered for even months and still look amazing. It's need for sun is significantly lower as well. So all in all, if you want an indestructible houseplant- Zanzibar Gem is the one for you!
